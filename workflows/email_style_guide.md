# Email Style Guide — NCAA Sports Outreach

## Voice & Persona

All outreach emails come from **Meire Medeiros, Catering Manager at Livite**. The tone should feel like a friendly, professional person who genuinely wants to help — not a sales machine.

**Key principles:**
- Warm but not overly familiar
- Concise — coaches are busy, respect their time
- Specific — reference the actual game, date, opponent, venue
- Low-pressure — "no pressure," "whenever you're ready," "happy to help"
- Action-oriented — always include a clear next step (menu link, call-to-action)

## Subject Lines

**Format:** `{School} {Sport} at {Home School}`

- Keep it simple and specific — the school + sport + location is enough
- No ALL CAPS, no exclamation marks, no emojis
- No generic subjects like "Catering Services" or "Hello from Livite"
- Follow-ups: `Following up — {School} {Sport}` or `Last note — {School} away games`

**Examples:**
- `Tufts Baseball at Harvard`
- `Following up — UMass Lacrosse`
- `Last note — Stonehill away games`

## Body Structure

### Cold Outreach (Step 1)

```
Hi {First Name},

[HOOK — reference the specific game]
I saw that {School} {Sport} is playing at {Home School} on {Date} and wanted to reach out.

[INTRO — who we are, one sentence]
I'm Meire, the Catering Manager at Livite — we're a fast-casual restaurant in the Boston area that works with a lot of college teams on game days.

[VALUE PROP — what we do for them, one sentence]
We handle pre-game and post-game meals delivered right to your bus so your staff doesn't have to deal with the logistics.

[CTA — soft ask]
If you're looking for meal support for that trip, I'd love to send over our catering menu with pricing.

[MENU LINK]
Here's our catering menu: https://www.livite.com/catering/livite

[CLOSE]
Feel free to reach out anytime — I'd love the chance to support your team.

[SIGNATURE]
```

```

Just following up on my last note — I know the season keeps you busy.

[SPECIFIC OFFER]
If you still need meal options for {School}'s trip to {Home School}, I can put together a quick quote based on your roster size. Most teams we work with order for 30-50 people, but we customize to fit.

[EASY CTA]
```

## Multi-Game Teams

When a team has multiple games in Boston, mention it naturally:

- **In subject:** Keep same format (reference one game)
- **In body:** Add: "I also noticed you have {N} games in the Boston area this season — happy to set up meals for any or all of them."
- **In template variable:** `{{other_games_list}}` provides: "March 3rd at Tufts, April 21st at UMass Boston"

## Response Handling Tone

### When someone is interested:
- Be enthusiastic but not over the top
- Move to logistics quickly (roster size, delivery time, menu preferences)
- Example: "That's great to hear! I'd love to put something together for your team."

### When someone declines:
- Gracious, brief, leave the door open
- Example: "Totally understand — thanks for letting me know. Feel free to reach out anytime if your plans change. Good luck this season!"

### When someone asks about pricing:
- Respond with specific options, not "it depends"
- Reference what similar teams order
- Example: "Most teams order individual boxes for 30-40 people, which runs about $X-$Y per person including delivery."

### When someone is out of office:
- Don't follow up immediately — wait for their return date + 2 days
- Reference original message when following up

## Do NOT

- Use emojis in initial outreach (Meire's real emails don't use them in outreach, only in operational/follow-up emails with existing clients)
- Say "I hope this email finds you well"
- Use corporate buzzwords (synergy, leverage, optimize)
- Mention competitors
- Send more than 3 emails in a sequence without a response
- Contact someone who previously said "not interested" — the system handles this automatically
- Use "Dear" — always "Hi {First Name}"
- Include pricing in cold outreach — let them ask
